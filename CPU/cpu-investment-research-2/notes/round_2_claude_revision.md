AI capex remains the dominant 2026 demand signal, but CPU dollars are a smaller and slower-growing slice than GPU/ASIC/HBM/networking/packaging. Merchant x86 grows mid-single to low-double digits in revenue, captive ARM continues to take new-capacity share at the top three U.S. hyperscalers, and OEM/ODM revenue grows faster than profit. **Bear Case — Medium confidence**
Accelerated racks, captive ARM CPUs at AWS/Azure/GCP, and ASIC racks (Broadcom-built TPU/Trainium/Maia) absorb the marginal new compute slot. Hyperscaler 2H26 capex digestion (CDR-37) hits OEM/ODM and merchant CPU first.

The bull, base, and bear cases all share the same fatal evidence gap: **CPU dollars per rack** (CDR-23). Until that table exists, no case is rigorously model-supported.
