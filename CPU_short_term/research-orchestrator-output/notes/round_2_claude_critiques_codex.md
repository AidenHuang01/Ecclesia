## 6. Logical gaps and causal errors

1. **Inconsistent evidentiary standard.** Codex marks "AMD May 5 implied move / skew" as "needs more evidence" but treats AMD RSI 88.95 as confirmed. Both come from the same category of secondary technical data. Either both are evidence-grade or neither is.
2. **The "Reject" dispositions are circular.** Codex rejects deletion of Intel Q1 numbers by saying "Intel's Q1 release confirms revenue $13.6B…" — but the question being challenged is precisely whether that "confirmation" is in the record. Re-asserting the conclusion is not a rebuttal.
3. **Mercury Research Q1 2026 server CPU share — feasibility issue ignored.** Mercury Research server-share data for a quarter typically lags by weeks. The trade decision is dated 2026-04-26; Q1 2026 data may not yet be published. Codex moves this to "needs more evidence" without acknowledging the data may not exist within the trade horizon. That changes the disposition from "we should look this up" to "this evidence is structurally unavailable for the decision window."
4. **"AMD watch item, not buy" preserves a residual AMD-over-INTC preference.** The new ranking has AMD #2 ("best watch item") and INTC #3. That is still a preference, dressed in passive language. If the catalyst cuts both ways at extreme RSI, the symmetric position is "neither is preferred ex ante; revisit only if AMD prints constructively." Codex has not earned even a soft AMD preference.
5. **No edge math, still.** The accepted-challenge table says "2%-3% AMD plan lacked edge math → Replaced with no-buy-now." Good — but the replacement also has no edge math. "Avoid" is the right call but the framework remains qualitative.
6. **Required deliverables not produced.** The prompt requires (a) the Required Final Table with eleven specific rows, (b) the seven required research-area tables, (c) explicit comparison vs DRAM and CPO trades. Round 2 produced none of these. Codex moved the conclusion in the right direction but did not deliver the format the investor asked for.
