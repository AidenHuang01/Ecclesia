## 1. Executive Summary

The CPU / agentic-AI narrative has *some* primary-source support — Intel management directly tied inference and agentic AI workloads to CPU/packaging demand on the Q1 2026 call, and Q1 DCAI revenue grew strongly. But the transcript repeatedly conflates **DCAI segment growth with Xeon CPU growth**, and **AMD's Data Center segment with EPYC**. Neither equivalence holds: both segments commingle CPUs with accelerators, networking, and other products. The agentic-AI revenue contribution is **not isolated in any disclosure**.

Both stocks have already moved violently (~+87% INTC and ~+69% AMD over one month per Barchart-sourced figures referenced in the debate). The technical setup is, by both agents' admission, stretched. The mandate's explicit decision rules — "do not chase," "expected upside meaningfully better than SMH," "downside controllable" — are not currently met for either name.

The two agents converged on **Avoid immediate entry; Watchlist only**, after Codex pushed Claude off a softer "AMD if forced" framing. That convergence is the right answer, and the evidence supports it.

**Verdict: Avoid / Watchlist.** Conditional re-evaluation only after AMD's May 5, 2026 print and a constructive technical reset.

## 8. Topic-Specific Analysis

### Is the CPU / Agentic AI Narrative Real?
- **Real as company commentary**, especially from Intel: management on the Q1 2026 call linked inference/agentic workloads to CPU and packaging demand. Intel also announced Xeon 6 as host CPU in NVIDIA DGX Rubin NVL8 — a data point for the host-CPU-attached-to-GPU sub-claim, but with a 2027+ revenue-relevant timeline, not Q2 2026.
- **Real but unisolated as revenue.** DCAI grew strongly, but DCAI bundles Xeon, accelerators, networking, NICs, IPUs, and custom ASICs. Neither Intel nor AMD has disclosed an "agentic-specific" revenue bridge. AMD's 39% YoY DC growth in Q4 commingles EPYC and Instinct.
- **Plausible but unproven as theme.** Agentic AI logically increases need for orchestration CPUs, host CPUs in GPU systems, and inference-suitable server CPUs. None of this has been quantified independently in the debate.

### Is the Stock Move Linked to the Narrative?
- **INTC: yes, partially.** The Apr 24 gap followed the Q1 print, and the Q1 commentary did include AI/CPU language. But INTC is also a turnaround, foundry, government-strategic, and margin-recovery story; CPU-specific contribution to the gap is not isolated.
- **AMD: weakly.** AMD had no AMD-specific CPU news on Apr 24. The move is best characterized as Intel sympathy + sector beta + AI-platform enthusiasm. Calling it a "CPU narrative" trade for AMD is interpretive, not evidenced.

### Is There Upside Left in 1–3 Months?
- **Bull path exists** (AMD May 5 confirms EPYC strength + raises guide + holds the reaction; hyperscaler capex commentary corroborates).
- **Bear path is more probable from current technicals** (post-+87%/+69% 1M moves on stretched RSI, AMD heading into earnings without an implied-move check, INTC fresh off a one-day gap).
- **Base case:** chop and partial retracement; SMH-like total return for the next 3 months with much higher single-name volatility.

### Which Stock Is Better?
- **Neither, ex ante.** AMD is the better *event* (binary May 5); INTC has the cleaner *narrative print* (Apr 23) but is post-catalyst.
- The "AMD is better" framing was correctly downgraded across both rounds.

### Entry / Exit / Risk Rules
- **Entry:** wait. If AMD May 5 confirms (EPYC-specific, raised FY, held reaction): starter ≤2% on a defined technical level (gap fill / 20-DMA / 50-DMA / breakout retest). For INTC: wait for level-based pullback + analyst *number* revisions (not just PT bumps).
- **Exit:** level-based; specific levels not pulled in this session.
- **Stop:** decisive break back into the pre-rally range, or thesis-invalidation event (EPYC guide-down, hyperscaler "CPU mix returning to normal" language, AMD Q1 margin guide-down).
- **Vehicle:** mandate prefers common shares; options check (implied move, skew, dealer gamma) was never executed and is a hard blocker for any options-vs-shares recommendation.

### Comparable to DRAM / CPO?
- **Weaker than DRAM / HBM** on revenue visibility. HBM has multi-quarter sold-out commentary from Micron / SK Hynix and observable contract pricing series. CPU does not have an equivalent revenue-pricing signal.
- **Unresolved vs CPO.** The debate never produced 2026-dated CPO revenue-ramp data (Marvell, Coherent, Broadcom, Lumentum). The CPO comparison should be reported as a **deliverable failure** for this memo, not a soft "comparable" claim.
- **CPU has a fresher single-stock catalyst (INTC Apr 23 print, AMD May 5 print) than CPO**, but catalyst freshness ≠ better trade edge after the move.
